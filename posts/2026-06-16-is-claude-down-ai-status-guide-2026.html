Quick Checks: How to Know if Claude is Down for Everyone or Just You

When Claude seems unresponsive, the first step is to diagnose if it’s a widespread outage or a local issue on your end. Panic is never a strategy. Instead, follow a structured approach to quickly pinpoint the problem. Knowing the difference can save you hours of futile troubleshooting.

Check Claude's Official Status Page First

Anthropic, like any major service provider, maintains a public status page that provides real-time updates on its systems. This is your go-to source for authoritative information.

  • Look for indicators like "Operational," "Degraded Performance," or "Service Outage" for core services (API, web interface, specific models).
  • Note the timestamp of the last update to ensure you have the most current information.
  • Subscribe to their notifications if available, so you get alerts directly via email or SMS when incidents occur or are resolved.

Consult Third-Party Monitors and Social Media

Sometimes, official pages take a moment to update. Independent monitoring sites and social media platforms can offer immediate, crowdsourced insights into widespread issues.

  • IsItDownRightNow.com or DownDetector: These sites aggregate user reports and often show a surge in complaints minutes after a service disruption begins. Just type "Anthropic Claude" into their search bar.
  • X (formerly Twitter): Search for "@AnthropicAI," "#ClaudeAI," or "is Claude down" to see if other users are reporting similar problems. Official accounts often post updates here first.

Test Your Own Connection and Application

Don't overlook the simple things. Sometimes the problem isn't Claude, but your own setup.

  • Internet Connection: Can you access other websites and online services? A quick speed test can confirm your network is stable.
  • Browser/App Cache: Clear your browser's cache and cookies, or try using a different browser. For desktop applications, restart the app.

Pro Tips for Uninterrupted AI Workflow (Even When Claude is Down)

Mastering AI workflow resilience isn't just about reacting to outages; it's about building a system that anticipates and minimizes their impact. Here are my top pro tips, forged from years of navigating the unpredictable world of AI services.

  • Diversify Your AI Stack: Relying on a single AI provider is a critical vulnerability. Maintain subscriptions or access to at least two major generative AI platforms (e.g., Claude and Gemini/ChatGPT) to quickly pivot during an outage. This is non-negotiable for serious users.
  • Design for API Resilience: If you're building applications that integrate with Claude's API, implement robust error handling, retry logic with exponential backoff, and circuit breakers. Don't let a temporary API blip crash your entire application.
  • Cache AI Responses Intelligently: For tasks where the AI response doesn't need to be unique every time (e.g., generating boilerplate text, common code snippets), cache previous valid responses. This reduces dependency on live API calls and speeds up your application.
  • Break Down Complex Prompts: Instead of sending one massive, intricate prompt, break it into smaller, sequential steps. If one step fails due to a temporary hiccup, you lose less work and can restart from the last successful step. This is also good prompt engineering practice.
  • Regularly Backup Critical Data: Any proprietary data you feed into Claude for fine-tuning or analysis should always have secure, independent backups. While service outages rarely mean data loss for users, it's about peace of mind.
  • Stay Informed via Multiple Channels: Don't just rely on email alerts. Follow official AI provider accounts on X, join relevant Discord/Slack communities, and regularly check their status pages. Redundancy in monitoring means quicker awareness.
