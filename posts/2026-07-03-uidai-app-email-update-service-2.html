Step-by-Step: Updating Your Email Through the Official mAadhaar App

The Process: mAadhaar App & Aadhaar Seva Kendra (ASK)

For security reasons, changing your registered email (or mobile number) requires a higher level of verification than a simple demographic update. UIDAI mandates an in-person visit to an Aadhaar Seva Kendra (ASK) or a designated bank/post office branch for this specific change. Here's how the mAadhaar app streamlines part of this crucial process:

  1. Download the mAadhaar App: If you haven't already, install the official mAadhaar app from Google Play Store or Apple App Store. Ensure it's the genuine app published by UIDAI.
  2. Register Your Aadhaar: Open the app and register your Aadhaar profile using your existing registered mobile number (which must be active). This allows you to manage various Aadhaar services.
  3. Verify Email (If Previously Registered): The app has a "Verify Email/Mobile" feature. Use this to confirm if your current email is already linked to your Aadhaar. If it's correct, you're all set! If it shows an old email or "not registered," then an update is needed.
  4. Locate Nearest ASK: Within the mAadhaar app, use the "Locate an Enrolment/Update Centre" feature. This is invaluable for finding the Aadhaar Seva Kendra closest to you, saving you time and effort.
  5. Book an Appointment (Optional but Recommended): Some ASKs allow you to book an appointment directly through the mAadhaar app or the UIDAI website. This significantly reduces waiting times. Select "Aadhaar Update," then "Email Update" (or demographic update), and choose your preferred time slot.
  6. Visit the Aadhaar Seva Kendra: Go to the chosen ASK on your appointment date with your Aadhaar card and a valid ID proof (for verification). You'll fill out an Aadhaar Enrolment/Update Form. Clearly indicate the new email address you wish to register.
  7. Biometric Verification & Fee Payment: The operator will update your details and perform a biometric verification (fingerprint/iris scan). A nominal fee will be charged for the update service.
  8. Receive Update Request Number (URN): After successful submission, you'll receive an Update Request Number. Keep this safe to track the status of your email update via the UIDAI website or the mAadhaar app.
  9. Monitor Status: The update typically takes a few days. You can track its progress using your URN. Once processed, your new email will be linked.

UIDAI Email Update: Pro Tips to Avoid Headaches and Ensure Success

Updating something as fundamental as your Aadhaar-linked email can seem daunting, but with a few insider tips, you can make the process smooth and error-free. Don't fall victim to common pitfalls that delay your updates and cause unnecessary frustration.

  • Always Use Your Primary, Active Email: Register an email you check regularly and plan to keep long-term. This avoids future update hassles and ensures you receive timely, critical notifications, much like how you'd master your messaging for other digital interactions.
  • Double-Check for Typos: Before submitting, meticulously review your new email address. A single typo can render the update useless and force you to restart the entire process. Operators can make mistakes too, so verify on screen.
  • Carry Original Documents: Even if you book online, always bring original copies of your Aadhaar card and any supporting ID proof mentioned for the update. Digital copies might not suffice for verification.
  • Understand the Fee Structure: Be aware that a nominal fee (usually around ₹50 as of 2026) is charged for demographic updates like email. Factor this into your planning.
  • Keep Your URN Secure: The Update Request Number (URN) is your golden ticket. It's the only way to track your request. Take a photo, write it down – just don't lose it.
  • Verify Post-Update: Once you receive confirmation or after a few days, use the mAadhaar app's "Verify Email/Mobile" feature to confirm that your new email has been successfully linked. This final check is crucial.
