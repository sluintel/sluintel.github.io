Mastering Midjourney Prompts: A Step-by-Step Guide

The key to extraordinary results with Midjourney lies in your prompts. It’s an art form in itself – prompt engineering. Here’s how to construct prompts that truly shine:

  1. Start with the Core Subject: Begin with the main subject you want to depict. Be specific. Instead of "a dog," try "a golden retriever puppy."
  2. Add Descriptive Adjectives & Verbs: Enhance the subject with details. "A golden retriever puppy, playfully chasing a butterfly."
  3. Define the Environment/Setting: Where is this happening? "A golden retriever puppy, playfully chasing a butterfly in a sun-drenched meadow at dawn."
  4. Specify Style & Artistic Influences: This is crucial for aesthetic control. "A golden retriever puppy, playfully chasing a butterfly in a sun-drenched meadow at dawn, photorealistic, dramatic lighting, high detail, by Annie Leibovitz." You can also add styles like "oil painting," "anime," "pixel art," etc.
  5. Utilize Parameters: These are powerful modifiers. They come after your main prompt, usually starting with two hyphens. Some common ones:
    • --ar 16:9 (aspect ratio for wide images)
    • --v 5.2 (specify the model version – always use the latest for best results)
    • --s 750 (stylize, controls how artistic the AI is – higher for more abstract, lower for more literal)
    • --no [element] (to exclude elements, e.g., --no trees)
    • --niji (for anime-style images)
  6. Experiment and Iterate: The best way to learn is by doing. Generate, observe, refine your prompt, and generate again. Small tweaks can make huge differences.

Pro Tips & Common Midjourney Mistakes to Avoid

You can go from good to great with Midjourney by understanding these nuances. Don't just generate; optimize.

  • Be Specific, Then Abstract: Start with clear, concrete ideas. Once you have a base, experiment with more abstract concepts, emotions, or philosophical ideas in your prompts.
  • Learn from Others: Spend time in the official Midjourney Discord channels. Observe what successful prompts look like and how others achieve their effects. It's a goldmine for learning.
  • Don't Over-Prompt: A common mistake is cramming too many conflicting ideas into one prompt. Keep it focused. If you want two distinct elements, consider generating them separately and compositing them later.
  • Embrace Randomness: Sometimes the most unexpected results are the best. Don't be afraid to let the AI surprise you, especially when exploring new themes.
  • Understand Negative Prompting: Using --no [element] is incredibly powerful. If you're getting unwanted objects, textures, or colors, explicitly tell Midjourney what to avoid.
  • Master the "Imagine" Command: Remember, every prompt starts with /imagine. Get into the habit of thinking about your prompt as a command, not just a description.
