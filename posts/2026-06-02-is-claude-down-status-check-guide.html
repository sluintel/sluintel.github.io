Troubleshooting When Claude AI Seems Down: Your Action Plan

If you've checked the status pages and there's no official report of an outage, the issue might be closer to home. Here's a step-by-step guide to troubleshoot common local problems:

  1. Check Your Internet Connection: It sounds obvious, but a shaky Wi-Fi or a disconnected ethernet cable is often the culprit. Try loading other websites or applications to confirm your internet is stable.

  2. Clear Browser Cache and Cookies: Stale data can sometimes interfere with web application functionality. Clear your browser's cache and cookies, then restart the browser and try accessing Claude again.

  3. Try a Different Browser or Incognito Mode: Browser extensions or specific browser settings can cause conflicts. Test Claude in a different web browser (e.g., Chrome, Firefox, Edge) or an incognito/private browsing window to rule out these issues.

  4. Restart Your Device: A simple reboot can resolve many underlying software glitches that might be preventing Claude from loading correctly.

  5. Check for Local Network Restrictions: If you're on a corporate or school network, firewalls or content filters might be blocking access to AI services. Consult your IT department if you suspect this is the case.

  6. Review Your API Keys/Access Tokens: If you're accessing Claude via an API, ensure your API key hasn't expired, been revoked, or reached its usage limits. Check your Anthropic developer dashboard.

  7. Contact Anthropic Support: If all else fails and you've confirmed it's not a widespread outage or a local issue you can resolve, reach out to Anthropic's support team directly. Provide as much detail as possible about your problem.
