Venezuela's Role in Global Energy Markets

Despite years of underinvestment and sanctions, Venezuela still possesses the world's largest proven oil reserves. Its potential, or lack thereof, to significantly increase oil production has major implications for a global market increasingly sensitive to supply disruptions.

Sanctions relief, even partial, can open doors for foreign investment and revitalize its struggling oil industry. Conversely, renewed sanctions or internal instability can send ripples through the market, affecting prices at your local gas station. Keeping an eye on Caracas means keeping an eye on your wallet, to a degree.
