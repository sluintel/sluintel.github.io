Crafting Effective Prompts: A Simple Framework

\n

I like to break down good prompts into a few key components:

\n
    \n
  1. \nDefine the Role: Tell Claude who it should be. "You are an expert SEO content strategist." "You are a friendly customer service agent." This sets the tone and context.
    2. \n
  2. \nState the Task Clearly: What exactly do you want it to do? "Write a 500-word blog post." "Summarise this document."
    3. \n
  3. \nProvide Context/Background: Give it all the necessary information. "The blog post should be about the benefits of remote work for small businesses." "The document discusses recent trends in renewable energy."
    4. \n
  4. \nSpecify Constraints/Format: How should the output look? "Use bullet points." "Keep it under 300 words." "Include a call to action." "Target a 7th-grade reading level."
    5. \n
  5. \nOffer Examples (Optional but Powerful): If you have a specific style in mind, show it an example. "Here's an example of the kind of tone I'm looking for..."
    6. \n
\n

For instance, instead of just saying "Write a blog post," try: "You are a humorous tech blogger. Write a 600-word blog post about the funniest AI fails. Use an informal, conversational tone. Include three distinct examples and a concluding thought about learning from mistakes." See the difference? You're setting Claude up for success.
