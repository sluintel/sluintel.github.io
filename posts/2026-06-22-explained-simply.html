Types of Pension Plans

Pensions generally fall into two main categories, each with distinct characteristics:

  • Defined Benefit (DB) Pensions: These are often older, employer-sponsored schemes where your retirement income is a predetermined amount, usually based on your salary and years of service. The employer takes on the investment risk. These are becoming less common but offer great security.
  • Defined Contribution (DC) Pensions: More common today, these plans involve you and/or your employer contributing a set amount into a personal investment pot. Your retirement income then depends on how much was contributed and how well the investments performed. You bear the investment risk, but also gain more control.
  • Government Pension Schemes: Many countries have state-provided pensions (like Social Security in some regions) that offer a basic income floor in retirement. These often supplement private pensions rather than fully replace them.
