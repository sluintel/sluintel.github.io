What often goes unsaid is the brutal efficiency required: a single off-day in the group stage can derail an entire campaign.

  • Group Stages: Teams face off against others in their assigned pool. Points awarded for wins, often with Net Run Rate (NRR) as the primary tie-breaker. This is where every single boundary and dot ball counts.
  • Knockout Rounds: The top performers from the groups advance. Semi-finals are win-or-go-home affairs, leading to the grand final. These are the matches that separate the contenders from the pretenders.
  • Qualifying Spots: The number of spots available for the main Asian Games event varies, but typically it’s the finalists who secure automatic berths. Sometimes, a bronze medal match offers an additional spot, adding another layer of drama.
