  2. Track Trending Hashtags Beyond the Obvious: While #BELvsSEN is a given, look for more specific, nuanced hashtags (e.g., #DeBruyneDilemma, #SenegalStrong) that indicate deeper discussions rather than general chatter.
  3. Analyze Engagement Metrics Beyond Likes: Pay attention to replies, quote tweets, and shares. A high number of replies often signifies genuine debate, while shares indicate broader agreement or cultural resonance (like a meme).
  4. Cross-Reference Platforms: A meme might go viral on TikTok, while a tactical breakdown thrives on Reddit or X. Verify if a reaction is isolated to one platform or gaining traction across several, indicating wider impact.
  5. Look for Consensus in Disagreement: Even when fans disagree vehemently, a common thread often emerges regarding the type of issue at hand (e.g., everyone agrees the referee had a tough game, even if they disagree on specific calls).
