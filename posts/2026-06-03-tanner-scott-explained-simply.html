Pro Tips for Understanding Relief Pitching and Common Mistakes

Don't fall into the trap of oversimplifying the complex art of relief pitching. Here are some insider tips and common mistakes to avoid when evaluating players like Tanner Scott:

  • Don't Overemphasize Saves: While important, a save stat often reflects opportunity more than pure skill. A setup man who consistently pitches a dominant 8th inning in tight games might be more valuable than a closer who racks up saves in less critical situations.
  • Look Beyond ERA for Relievers: ERA can be volatile for relievers due to small sample sizes and bad luck. Focus more on xFIP, K/9, and WHIP for a truer measure of their underlying talent.
  • Understand the 'Leverage' Factor: A pitcher giving up a run in a low-leverage situation isn't the same as one doing it in a high-leverage scenario. Context is everything.
  • Beware of the Blown Save Trap: A blown save doesn't always mean a bad outing. The pitcher might inherit runners or give up a single run while still striking out the side. Dig deeper into the play-by-play.
  • Pay Attention to Inning Workload: Heavy usage can lead to fatigue or injury. A smart manager protects their elite arms, ensuring their 'automation' doesn't break down from overuse.
