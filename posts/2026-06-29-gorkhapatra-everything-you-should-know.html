What Is गोरखापत्र & Why It Still Commands Respect?

गोरखापत्र isn't just another newspaper; it's a national institution. Established in 1901 (B.S. 1958), it holds the distinction of being Nepal's oldest continually published newspaper. Initially a weekly, it evolved into a daily, becoming the official gazette and primary news outlet for the government. Today, it serves as a critical bridge between the state and its citizens, delivering official announcements, policy updates, and comprehensive national news.

  • Official Voice: It's the primary medium for government notices, laws, and public information.
  • Historical Record: A vital archive for scholars, historians, and citizens tracking Nepal's socio-political journey.
  • Cultural Barometer: Reflects national discourse, cultural events, and literary contributions over a century.
  • Multilingual Reach: While primarily in Nepali, it has sections or sister publications in other national languages, broadening its accessibility.

Unpacking the Enduring Significance of गोरखापत्र

For over a century, Gorakhapatra has been the authoritative voice for government decisions. From budget allocations and new legislation to presidential decrees and public service announcements, if it's official and from the Nepali government, you'll find it here first. This makes it an indispensable source for legal professionals, academic researchers, and citizens needing reliable, undisputed public records of parliamentary proceedings, court decisions, and government job vacancies. Its role in maintaining transparency for national governance cannot be overstated.

It publishes insightful articles on Nepali history, promotes local arts, and even features dedicated sections aimed at students preparing for competitive exams or exploring literary works. Columns by prominent Nepali writers, poets, and thinkers frequently appear, making it a unique blend of national record and vibrant community engagement. Its influence extends deeply into shaping public discourse on national identity, cultural preservation, and educational advancement.
