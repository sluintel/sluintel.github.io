  2. Understand Key Metrics:

    • P/E Ratio (Price-to-Earnings): This tells you how much investors are willing to pay for each rupee of earnings. A high P/E might suggest investors expect high future growth, but it could also mean the stock is overvalued.
    • EPS (Earnings Per Share): A company's profit divided by its outstanding shares. A rising EPS is generally a good sign.
    • Market Capitalization: The total value of all its shares. It tells you the company's size.
    • Dividend Yield: If you're an income investor, this shows the annual dividend payment relative to the share price.
